This is the CANCEL-07 proof.

Test A — cancel races cashout on the same pending wager:
- Seed funded wallet, open market, pending wager (amount 100, oddsAtTime 2, cashedOutAmount 0).
- `Promise.allSettled([ wagerService.cancelWager(wagerId, userId), wagerService.cashoutWager(wagerId, userId, { amount: 30, idempotencyKey: 'race-key' }) ])`.
- Assert the two operations cannot leave inconsistent money state: either the cancel wins (wager 'refunded', no wager_cashouts row, exactly one 'refund' wallet_transactions row and no 'credit' cashout row) OR the cashout wins first (a wager_cashouts row exists and the cancel then fails because cashed_out_amount > 0 -> ConflictError, wager stays 'pending'). Assert that NOT both a full 'refund' and a cashout credit landed on the same stake (no double-pay), and that the market->wager lock ordering did not deadlock (neither leg rejects with a raw 40P01/deadlock error — if a rejection occurs its constructor is ConflictError/ValidationError, an operational error).
- Reconcile: final wallet balance equals seeded balance plus exactly the net of whichever single operation committed.

Test B — cancel races market resolution:
- Seed funded wallet, open market, pending wager.
- Race `wagerService.cancelWager(wagerId, userId)` against `marketService.resolveMarket(...)` (import marketService; use its real signature — resolve the market to an outcome). Because both lock the market row first, Postgres serializes them.
- Assert exactly one outcome wins: either the wager is 'refunded' by cancel (and resolution skipped/settled it as already-refunded) or the wager was resolved (won/lost) and the cancel then fails with ConflictError because the wager is no longer 'pending'. Assert no double credit to the wallet and no deadlock error surfaces.

Keep assertions tolerant of either winner (the guarantee is "never both / never double-pay / never deadlock", not a fixed winner). Add the same header comment style as cashout.concurrency.test.js explaining why Promise.allSettled (not sequential awaits) is required.
